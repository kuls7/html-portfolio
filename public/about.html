About Me

How I started and struggled:

Hi my name is Kulsoom Midhat. I have a bachelors degree in Computer Science Engineering. I have since then, been trying to accomplish a full understanding of basic programming languages. Yes, I learned a bit in my bachelors, and no they do not teach you the basics or fundamentals. I never studied Computer Science before University so I barely caught up with what was being taught.
However, I didn't want to give up and especially when I found myself keen and excited about one course which I fortunately chose as an elective but ended up loving- Web Development. As much as I hated all other coding languages, I wanted to keep going just because of this slight spark I felt with this course.

More struggle:

After I miraculously graduated with a magna cum laude, I tried doing self-teaching by tricking myself into thinking I could achieve better if I tutored. So I invited all of my below average classmates over to my house and taught them the algorithms and data structures and a little bit of c++ and java here and there. Although it worked initially, as in I was able to grasp a lot of theories and concepts, I was not able to put efforts into building any projects myself. I lacked motivation and lost all the self esteem.
Few months later, I started tecahing as a professor at a Technical and Vocational College run by the Saudi Government. I did not teach a single thing about coding but rather the technical stuff like how to build a computer and how to update RAM. All the practical stuff with lab work. It wasn't exactly what I needed in my career but hey it was a start. I went on for about 2 years and mind you, I kept going farther and farther away from my goals. I kept getting rejected at other job interviews because of the zero knowledge and projects I had, and I wasn't even trying to find a solution. I took a break from work because I was getting married and moving countries. And this is where I found my energy back.

Ok but what did I do?

I had all these random courses I bought on Udemy like 3 years ago and I had forgotten all about it. When I moved to America, I knew getting started here would be way tougher beause of culture shock and academic standards. I decided to enroll in a masters program but i knew it would be like history repeating itself with me not knowing any fundamentals and basics of programming.
